Piedmont, Italy · A long weekend

Turin, at an unhurried pace.

Baroque arcades, uncommonly good chocolate, and the Alps on the horizon: a short guide to one of Italy's most quietly magnetic cities.

Start the 48-hour route

A city of details

Come for the museums. Stay for the daily ritual of the aperitivo.

Turin rewards wandering. Its grand boulevards lead to small coffee counters, former factories host contemporary art, and every view seems to frame the Mole Antonelliana.

Best time
April–June and September–October

The short version

Your first day in Turin.

09:00 — Centro

Breakfast with a bicerin

Order the warm chocolate-coffee-cream drink that Turin made famous, with a small pastry on the side.

11:00 — Via Accademia

Lose track of time at Museo Egizio

Book a timed entry in advance, particularly on weekends and school holidays.

16:30 — Quadrilatero Romano

Browse, then linger for aperitivo

The old Roman quarter is ideal for a late-afternoon glass of vermouth and a plate of local cheeses.
